Water Quality Standards Guide

How standards and reference lines are determined and displayed in this dashboard

Colorado Regulatory Framework

Water quality standards in Colorado are set by the Water Quality Control Commission (WQCC) and administered by CDPHE. For the San Miguel watershed, three regulations are most relevant:

  • Regulation 31 — Basic Standards and Methodologies for Surface Water. Contains statewide numeric tables used across all basins, including Table I (water supply standards), Table II (fixed inorganic standards), and Table III (hardness-based metal equations).
    → 5 CCR 1002-31 (PDF — CO Secretary of State)
  • Regulation 35 — Classifications and Numeric Standards for the Gunnison and Lower Dolores River Basins. Assigns designated use classifications and numeric standards to specific stream segments (identified by AUID code, e.g. COGUSM03b_A).
    → 5 CCR 1002-35 (PDF — CO Secretary of State)
  • Regulation 93 — Colorado's Section 303(d) List of Impaired Waters. Identifies segments where water quality standards are not being met and TMDLs are required.
    → 5 CCR 1002-93 (PDF — CO Secretary of State)

TVS — Hardness-Based Metal Standards

Many metals in Regulation 35 are listed as TVS (Table Value Standard). This means the numeric standard is not a fixed number — it is calculated from the water's hardness using equations in Regulation 31, Table III. Higher hardness (calcium and magnesium content, expressed as mg/L CaCO₃) results in a higher allowable concentration, reflecting reduced bioavailability of metals in harder water.

Hardness-based parameters in this dashboard: Zinc, Cadmium, Copper, Lead, Manganese, Uranium, Aluminum, Nickel, Chromium III.

How exceedances are determined here: For each metal measurement, we find the hardness measured at the same site within ±7 days, calculate the TVS using the Reg 31 Table III equation, and flag if the measured value exceeds that result. Where same-day hardness is unavailable, the site's median measured hardness is used, then the segment median as a fallback. The source of hardness used (same-day, nearby, site median, or segment median) is noted per measurement.

The dashed threshold lines on charts show the TVS at the segment's median hardness for visual reference.
Important: These are approximations for dashboard purposes. Formal compliance determinations under Regulation 35 require hardness measured at the same site and time as the metal sample.

→ CDPHE Hardness-Based Standards Calculator (Excel)

Arsenic: Aquatic Life vs. Water Supply Standard

Regulatory note: Regulation 35 includes a chronic water supply arsenic standard of 0.02 µg/L (total recoverable). This extremely low value is a known regulatory artifact — an artifact of how the water supply classification standard was written — and does not reflect ambient aquatic conditions. CDPHE has acknowledged this issue.

This dashboard uses the aquatic life standard (360 µg/L acute dissolved; 150 µg/L chronic dissolved) for all visualization and exceedance flagging. Where Agriculture use is designated (most San Miguel segments), the Agriculture standard of 100 µg/L total recoverable (more restrictive) also applies for acute comparison. The 0.02 µg/L value is retained in the underlying database for reference only.

Idarado Consent Decree Zinc Standard

Sites on the San Miguel mainstem from the Pandora Mill (Telluride) downstream to the South Fork confluence are subject to a separate compliance target from the 1987 Idarado Record of Decision, in addition to the state TVS zinc standard:

Zinc: 0.15 mg/L (150 µg/L) dissolved — compliance target per Table 4.1, Record of Decision for the Idarado Mining and Milling Complex (State of Colorado v. Idarado Mining Co., Civil Action 83-C-2385, March 1987). Applies to segments COGUSM03a_A and COGUSM03b_A.

This standard is shown as a separate dashed line (amber/brown color) on charts for applicable sites, clearly distinguished from the state TVS standard.

Nutrient Standards and EPA Reference Criteria

Nitrate, Nitrite, and Ammonia have numeric standards in Regulation 35 and are shown with enforceable threshold lines.

Total Nitrogen and Total Phosphorus do not have numeric standards in Regulation 35 — only a narrative standard applies in Colorado. This dashboard displays EPA 304(a) recommended reference condition criteria as context:

These are not enforceable standards. They represent the 25th percentile of minimally impacted reference streams in each ecoregion — a benchmark for what clean conditions look like, not a regulatory threshold. Reference lines are labeled "EPA Reference Condition" in charts.

The San Miguel watershed spans two EPA Aggregate Nutrient Ecoregions (based on EPA 822-B-00-015 and 822-B-00-016, December 2000):

  • Upper watershed — Ecoregion II (Western Forested Mountains / Southern Rockies): TP reference = 10 µg/L; TN reference = 0.12 mg/L. Segments COGUSM01–09, 14, 19, 20.
  • Lower watershed — Ecoregion III (Xeric West / Colorado Plateaus): TP reference = 21.88 µg/L; TN reference = 0.38 mg/L. Segments COGUSM10–12, COGULD.
